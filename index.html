The Origin: Why Mechanical?

I started in science because I wanted to understand how things work at a fundamental level. I chose Mechanical Engineering because it turns that curiosity into real, tangible systems you can design, build, and validate.

During my undergrad, I realized that I didn't just want to create "things"‚ÄîI wanted to create systems that solve critical problems. This foundation in mechanics, thermodynamics, and manufacturing gave me the toolkit to do exactly that.

Visual: Undergrad Capstone / Mechanical Workshop Photo

The Pivot: Medical Devices

The turning point happened during a hospital visit. Watching a CT Scanner in operation, I became fascinated by the precision required to diagnose a patient. It wasn't just biology; it was engineering.

I chose to pursue my Masters at Stevens Institute of Technology to specialize specifically in Medical Device Design. I wanted to bridge the gap between "working prototype" and "FDA-compliant product," focusing on quality systems, reliability, and manufacturability.
